Colour in Lumi is shaped by traditional media practice. Instead of treating colour as a purely abstract RGB problem, Lumi puts strong emphasis on pigments, palette structure, and mixing behaviour that feels useful to painters.

This helps artists build controlled palettes, make deliberate colour decisions, and work with harmonies that stay understandable across complex illustrations.
